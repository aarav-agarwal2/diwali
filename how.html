Now you must be wondering, what is an 'Eco-Friendly Diwali'? It is where you do not pollute the environment, but still enjoy Diwali. There are many ways to do that. Find the steps below:

  • You can make crackers from waste paper. The best thing about them is that they don't pollute the environment as there is no need to burn them.
  • You can talk to your relatives, have a video chat with them if you can't meet them.
  • You can call your friends home, and have a party.
  • You can also donate your old clothes, books, toys, etc. that you do not use to a organisation for the needy, or just give it to them on this auspicious day.
  • You can distribute sweets to your neighbours, friends, and your relatives.
  • You can take a vow to plant two (or how many you want) trees each year to make Earth free of pollution.

These were just some of the things that you can do. Just remember, do not do any thing that causes pollution. We want to get rid of it, not fill the Earth with it...
